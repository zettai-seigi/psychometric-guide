# Chapter 30: Personality Assessment Comparison

{: .note }
> **Note:** This chapter is currently under development. Content for Part VII (Comparative Analysis) will be added in a future update.

## Learning Objectives

- Compare OPQ32r with competing personality assessments
- Analyze methodological differences across providers
- Evaluate forced-choice vs. Likert-scale formats
- Understand competitive advantages of different approaches
- Assess validity evidence across personality measures

## Key Takeaways

- Major personality assessments (OPQ32r, Hogan, NEO-PI-R, 16PF) differ in format and methodology
- Forced-choice formats (OPQ32r) offer faking resistance advantages
- Likert-scale formats offer interpretive simplicity
- Thurstonian IRT represents significant methodological advancement
- Validity evidence should guide assessment selection decisions
