## Big Five Personality Factors

The Five Factor Model (Big Five) represents the most widely accepted model of personality structure:

1. **Extraversion** - Sociability, assertiveness, energy
2. **Conscientiousness** - Organization, dependability, achievement-striving
3. **Agreeableness** - Cooperation, empathy, consideration
4. **Emotional Stability** (vs. Neuroticism) - Calm, resilient, confident
5. **Openness to Experience** - Creativity, flexibility, intellectual curiosity

## OPQ32 Alignment

Research has demonstrated that:
- The OPQ32's structure is congruent with the Big Five factors
- Factor-analytic studies confirm this alignment
- 25 of the 32 OPQ scales map to the Five Factor Model using weighted composites
- The remaining traits capture workplace-specific constructs beyond the Big Five

## Theoretical Significance

The alignment with the Big Five:
- Provides theoretical grounding for the OPQ32
- Confirms construct validity
- Enables comparison with other personality measures
- Demonstrates that the OPQ32 measures established personality dimensions while adding workplace-relevant specificity

## Key Takeaways

1. The OPQ32's structure aligns with the scientifically validated Big Five personality model
2. This alignment provides strong evidence for construct validity
3. The OPQ32 extends beyond the Big Five to include additional workplace-relevant traits
4. Weighted composites enable calculation of Big Five scores from OPQ32 data
5. The theoretical convergence supports the scientific foundation of the OPQ32
